Alexander Hamilton

One of the Founding Fathers of the United States

Here's a timeline of Alexander Hamilton's life:

  • 1755 or 1757 - Born out of wedlock in Charlestown, Nevis (Caribbean Sea). Later orphaned and taken in by a prosperous merchant.
  • 1772 - Arrived by ship in Boston
  • 1773 - Entered King's College in New York City first as a private student and officially matriculated a year later
  • 1774 - Published his first political writings, A Full Vindication of the Measures of Congress and The Farmer Refuted .
  • 1775 - Took an early role in militia at the start of the American Revolutionary War.
  • 1777 - Became a senior aide to General Washington.
  • 1780 - Married Elizabeth Schuyler, a daughter of General Philip Schuyler.
  • 1782 - Birth of first son Philip Hamilton.
  • 1783 - Elected as a representative from New York to the Congress of the Confederation.
  • 1786 - Helped ratify the Constitution by writing 51 of the 85 installments of the Federalist Papers.
  • 1789 - Appointed as the first United States secretary of the treasury by President George Washington.
  • 1801 - Death of first son Philip in a duel.
  • 1804 - Campaigned against Aaron Burr as unworthy.
  • July 11, 1804 - Shot and mortally wounded by Burr in a dual. Later died the following day.
